Overview

In order to showcase my work, I needed to build a portfolio! Creating my portfolio turned out to be quite a process and I went through several iterations till I found one that satisfied me. I think that is why building my portfolio is so fun because it is a project that never ends and continues to grow with me. I also really enjoyed developing it with HTML, CSS, and jQuery.

Client

Myself

Role

UX, Wireframe, UI, Development

Process

Research, Wireframe, Style Tiles, High Fidelity Mockups, Development

The Goal

Create and develop an online portfolio for my work.

UX

In order to build my portfolio I first looked to designers I admired and see how they organized their portfolios.

From doing this I gathered that:

  • - What content I should and should not have on my portfolio
  • - Different ways of organizing my content
  • - Make my portfolio and extension of myself, allow it to showcase who I am as a person
  • - Rather than showing my final design, I noticed it would be better to explain my design thinking process and represent my projects as case studies so the person viewing my project would have a better understanding as to how I made my design decisions and came to the final design.

Wireframes

After researching portfolios, I began to mockup a wireframe in Sketch of how I would like my portfolio to look.

It made sense to include the navigation at the top and then have it positioned fixed in CSS so the user could navigate easily once on the page. I then wanted to include the about section next so the user would understand the purpose of the site and get a brief intro to me as a person.

Style Tiles

Now that I had my wireframe laid out I created some style tiles. I wanted a somewhat elegant and modern look.

High Fidelity Mockups

At first I had wanted to go with the pink, black, and grey theme and a created high fidelity mock up. However, I felt the background image could be better and the overall design could use refinement so explored more imagery and colors and created the second theme. At first I was satisfied with the second theme, and I even coded the entire site. However, once I finished coding, I felt that the design was too dark and was not as elegant looking as I would like it to be. I researched and looked into more design inspiration, colors, and typography and came up with the last theme.
